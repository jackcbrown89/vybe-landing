Collection of Information

Information You Provide to Us

We collect information you provide directly to us. For example, we collect information you provide when you create an account, post comments or otherwise participate in any interactive features of the Service, fill out a form, participate in a contest or promotion, apply for a job, communicate with us via third party social media sites, request customer support or otherwise communicate with us. Information We Collect Automatically When You Use the Service

When you access or use our Service, we automatically collect information about you, including:

Log Information: We log information about your use of the Service, including the type of browser you use, access times, pages viewed, your IP address and the page, site or other source that referred you to our Service.

Device Information: We collect information about the computer, mobile device, or other consumer electronic device you use to access our Service, including the hardware model, operating system and version, device identifiers and mobile network information.

Location Information: We may collect general information about your location which we derive from your IP address. We may also collect specific information about the location of your device when you access or use one of our applications, but only with your consent . For more details, please see "Your Choices" below.

Information Collected by Cookies and Other Tracking Technologies: We use various technologies to collect information about your use of the Service, including the music you listen to and your listening activity (including how long you listen to songs, what songs you skip, what songs you like or dislike, and how you swipe songs) and this may include sending cookies or other tracking devices to your computer or mobile device. Cookies are small data files stored on your hard drive or in device memory that help us to make recommendations and improve our Service and your experience, see which areas and features of our Service are popular and count visits. We may also collect information using web beacons (also known as "tracking pixels"). Web beacons are electronic images that may be used in our Service or emails and help deliver cookies, count visits, understand usage and campaign effectiveness and determine whether an email has been opened and acted upon. For more information about cookies, and how to disable them, please see "Your Choices" below.
